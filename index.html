If you're reading this, you've successfully connected to the internet.

What's this?

When you connect to a new network, often you have to log in first. Unfortunately your web browser doesn't always realize this and shows you an error instead, because many sites make sure that you're connecting directly and securely to the place you typed into your address bar.

By typing letmelog.in into your browser, you'll almost always be sent directly to the login page you're looking for instead. We pledge never to use HTTPS or HSTS — these are the technologies that confuse your browser when you try to browse normally.

Read more about captive portals and HTTPS on Wikipedia.
